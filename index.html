The product

A Claude-powered learning companion that starts with what you already know.

Navigator runs a 30-minute Socratic onboarding to map your real expertise, target role, and time budget — then generates a phased, ordered learning plan that updates weekly as you progress.

1. Diagnose, don't quiz

An open-ended Claude conversation maps what you actually know — not multiple choice. Output: a structured "fluency map" across literacy, hands-on, and safety dimensions.

2. Anchor + supplement

Anthropic Academy is the spine. Navigator pulls in DeepLearning.AI, BlueDot, books, and projects only where they close real gaps you have for your target.

3. Ship something every phase

Each phase ends in a public artifact — a writeup, a prototype, an eval suite. The portfolio compounds. By month 9 you have proof, not just credentials.

What makes this an Anthropic product, not a general-AI one

  • Socratic by default. Claude's pedagogical mode is the right substrate for adults who hate being lectured. Same philosophy as Learning Mode.
  • Honesty over completion. Most ed-tech optimizes for finish rates. Navigator surfaces gaps loudly and tells you when something on the path is genuinely optional.
  • Safety isn't a module — it's woven through. Every phase has a safety lens, because an AI-fluent professional who hasn't thought about alignment is a liability, not an asset.

Success metrics

North star: % of learners who ship 1 portfolio artifact in 90 days
Engagement: Weeks-active out of first 12
Honesty: % of plans where Navigator removed an item the user thought they wanted
Anthropic flywheel: Anthropic Academy course completions sourced from Navigator

Guardrail metrics (the non-obvious ones)

Trust score: "Did Navigator ever recommend something that wasted your time?" — keep this falling
Pace honesty: Median delta between planned hrs/week and actual — if >30%, plans are dishonest
Safety surface area: % of learners who can articulate a non-canned alignment perspective by phase 2

Risks & mitigations

RiskMitigation
Onboarding is too long for senior prosTest 30 vs 15 min Socratic flows; optimize for "felt heard" not "covered everything"
Anchor library feels self-servingSurface non-Anthropic resources prominently when they're best-in-class. Trust > lock-in.
Plans become staleWeekly check-in is a hard product requirement, not an opt-in
Sycophantic Claude tells learners what they want to hearBuild "honest pushback" into the system prompt; eval for it explicitly

The moat (why this is defensible)

It isn't features. Anyone can ship a quiz that produces a learning plan. The moat is trust — built through honest pushback, by being willing to tell learners that the thing they think they need to learn is actually a distraction. That's an Anthropic-shaped advantage, not a generic LLM one.
